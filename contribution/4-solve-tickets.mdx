# 🔨 Solve Tickets

Feedback and a healthy discussion culture are essential to any collaboration. 

To create an easy and fast entrance into our IaD world we want to foster and moderate this feedback by **providing Templates to raise Bugs and Feature requests in a similar process across all components**, allowing contributors to support in various projects easily.

You are missing a feature or spotted a Bug? Get involved and help us make Infrastructure as Data step by step better and stretching in to new areas.

With open-source we want to:

* Promotes reuse and collaboration
* Increases the quality of code and documentation
* Enables cross-team and organization innovation
* Giving everybody access and the opportunity shape the future of creating and managing infrastructure in the cloud

## Types of projects
There are different types of project we want to share for collaboration.
Let's reiterate their purpose to jointly move in a shared direction.

### Shared Documentation
The Shared Documentation is the a place where we want to share best practices, guides and other helpful information that can be used across all our projects. It is not meant to be a place for project specific documentation, but rather a place for general information and best practices that can be applied to all projects.

In case of typos, confusing guides, missing information, or you think an entire topic is missing please don't hesitate and [submit an improvement](#contribute-as-creator). We are happy about any contribution to make our documentation better and more helpful for everyone.

The shared documentation Repository can be found here: [crossplane-provider-docs](https://github.com/SAP/crossplane-provider-docs)

## Contribution steps

:::note

We would love to see you contributing to our projects, no matter if it's a small typo in the documentation or a new feature in one of our providers. Every contribution is welcome and helps us to make our projects better.
If you are interested in contributing, please check out the respective repository and get in touch with us. We are happy to support you in your contribution journey.

:::

### Contribute as reviewer
We like to develop software that really solves the problems of our users. To ensure this, we need your feedback on our work. That means very one is welcome to comment on open PRs and Issues, give feedback and share their opinion.
Do to different access rights, to be a reviewer who can approve PRs, you need to be a member of this repository's team, if you are interested in becoming a reviewer, please get in touch with us.

### Contribute as creator

For additional rights, to **open PRs and Branches**, you can request the role **Collaborator**.

1. Ensure you have role **Collaborator** or are a member of the github team owning this **repository**
1. Create new a new branch from `main`, e.g. `feat/addNewResourceXYZ`
1. Work on your contribution and get in touch with us in case you need support
1. After finalizing your contribution create a `pull request` against `main`
1. In the pull request, document your changes including:
    - Reason for contribution or change
    - Description of feature and the use cases / solved requirements
    - Determine suitable reviewers for your change
1. Your contribution will be reviewed (and hopefully approved) by the maintainers of the project team

## Bugs
If you spot an error or misbehavior in any of the components, please report it using the **Bug template which is available in the specific repository of the provider/helper/documentation.**

![Create Bug in UI](/img/create_issue.gif)

### Bug flow
1. **Create** Bug using the template 
    1. Please use the built-in search and look for possible duplicates
2. An Owner of the repository, or its contributors, will **review** and provide Feedback, everyone is welcome to join the discussion and share their opinion.
    1. *Valid*: Added to backlog or linked to existing bugs
    1. *Invalid*: Closed, open for discussion, reopen if applicable.
    1. *More feedback required*: The issue will stay open, for sometime and will have an `author-action needed` label, to indicate that more information is needed from the issuer to draw a conclusion.
3. Task will be picked up by **an contributor** (_maybe it's even your self_) and **merged into `main`.
4. On completion, Backlog Items as well as GitHub issues will be marked as Done by Contributor.

:::tip

To stay up-to-date we recommend using **Github's [notification subscription](https://docs.github.com/en/account-and-profile/managing-subscriptions-and-notifications-on-github/setting-up-notifications/about-notifications).**

:::

## Feature Requests
If you believe **a feature is missing in shared components**, please do not hesitate and **get in touch with the community** of the repository.

### Feature Requests flow
1. **Create** Feature Request using the template provided in the specific repository of the provider/helper/documentation.
    1. Please use the built-in search and look for possible duplicates upfront
2. An Owner of the repository, or its contributors, will **review** and provide Feedback, everyone is welcome to join the discussion and share their opinion.
    1. *Valid*: Added to backlog or linked to existing bugs
    1. *Invalid*: Closed, open for discussion, reopen if applicable.
    1. *More feedback required*: The issue will stay open, for sometime and will have an `author-action needed` label, to indicate that more information is needed from the issuer to draw a conclusion.
3. Task will be picked up by **an contributor** (_maybe it's even your self_) and **merged into `main`.
4. On completion, Backlog Items as well as GitHub issues will be marked as Done by Contributor.

## Bug fix
If you want to **directly contribute a fix for a Bug**, please follow the steps below:

1. Assign the Bug to yourself, to indicate that you are working on it
2. Move the Issue to **"In Progress"** in the Project board
3. **Create a new branch** from `main`, e.g. `fix/issue-123-description`
4. Do important work on your fix 🔧
5. After finalizing your fix create a `pull request` against `main`
6. In the pull request, document your changes including:
    - Reason for contribution or change
    - Description of feature and the use cases / solved requirements
    - Determine suitable reviewers for your change (Feel free to reach out to us if you need help)
    - Reference the original Bug issue
6. Check if your contribution meets the [**Definition of Done**](/docs/contribution/solve-tickets#definition-of-done) above and all tests are **passing**
7. Create a release candidate (check out our guide here for this: [Release process](/docs/contribution/4-solve-tickets.mdx#create-release-candidate))
8. Get in contact with the issuer of the Bug to validate your fix 💬
